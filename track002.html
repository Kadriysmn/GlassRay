Solution Steps
✓ Step 1 — Snell's Law at Critical Angle
Write Snell's Law at the boundary when i₂ = 90°:
n₁ · sin(θc) = n₂ · sin(90°)
Since sin(90°) = 1, this simplifies to:
n₁ · sin(θc) = n₂
✓ Step 2 — Why TIR requires n₁ > n₂
For TIR to occur, sin(i₂) > 1 must have no real solution.
This is only possible when light travels from a denser medium (n₁) to a less dense medium (n₂) — i.e. n₁ > n₂. Otherwise the refracted angle always has a valid solution.
